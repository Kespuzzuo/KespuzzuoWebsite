Ditching Spotify?

November 18, 2024

Now that I'm using RYM, I think I might make the move and use RYM as the main method for telling people what I've been listening to. It has a "Currently listening to" feature, with a history telling what I listened to. There's also lists, which fulfill the purpose of the playlists I make (logging the music I listen to during a certain period of time)

I already took all of my 2022 playlists and put them on a big yearly list of music, 2023 and 2024 still need to be added.

RYM also gives me more, I could listen to a song on any platform and still log it there. I can review an album or single and let people know about it. I can customize my profile even more. I can play around with lists to do even more than being playlists. It just feels more complete.

Also, I've been getting the idea that Spotify feels shady, and on top of that the page's been getting some bugs. Sometimes it wipes out the "liked song" indicator, and when I get back on the app, I have to click on the song's timeline to let Spotify know that I started listening to the song. It just feels janky.

I might miss Spotify Wrapped on the following years, but, I think it's not worth it. Last time I remember it was only available on the mobile app. I think wrapped is not worth it anymore, and that whatever I do on RYM has more weight than it.

So the plan is to make the December 2024 the last Spotify playlist, and then move onto just using RYM to log (and review) music.

Finished Kirby Star Allies

November 10, 2024

Yup, I finished that one. I finished Return to Dreamland (Deluxe) a year ago. I recall finishing Triple Deluxe two years ago, because of course I had to get into that Floralian lore. I finished Planet Robobot earlier this year. And, I just finished Star Allies.

So many people tend to put this game in a lower rank than the other Kirby games directed by Kumazaki. I think I get the reason why. The three previous games felt like they had a bigger scale, and each one had a bigger story going on. Robobot had the robot theming going on through all the stages, and each stage was like a spectacle on its own. Star Allies... while it still had some interesting stuff going on, like the lore of the ancients, it felt like it wasn't the next step after the previous games, but it was still fun.

On top of that there were the puzzle pieces, that replaces the "collect enough energy spheres, sun stones, or code cubes" collect system that the previous three games had it. That gameplay element from the previous three games was so good, that Pizza Tower decided to have it as well. In that old collectable system, I felt motivated to look through every place, and each red star door, so I could find other paths and collectables. The puzzle pieces feel different, because they're not a requirement to unlock the boss of each world, so there's less of a motivation to get them. Perhaps I should have established a self-imposed challenge of collecting all the rainbow puzzle pieces before fighting each boss...

I found it charming that you could have a lot of characters fighting alongside you, including the dream friends. The art style is also good, like the 3DS games, but in HD!

Some people might be asking themselves why I played it. The reason is that I'm going through all of the main modern Kirby games, since I like the way how Kumazaki shapes the lore, and I make Kirby (and Lolo) fan content, so I gotta know about that!

With Star Allies done, I will finally get to enjoy Kirby and the Forgotten Land, I heard it's amazing!
